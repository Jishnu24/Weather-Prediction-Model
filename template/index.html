About Rain Prediction Model

The Rain Prediction Model is a web application powered by a Machine Learning algorithm, designed to forecast whether it will rain the following day. Developed using the Rain Prediction in Australia dataset, the model operates behind the scenes to provide accurate predictions. The project's code repository can be accessed on my GitHub here. Australia experiences varying rainfall patterns, with over 80% of its landmass receiving less than 600 mm of rainfall annually, making it one of the driest continents after Antarctica. Inland areas, such as those near Lake Eyre, may receive as little as 81 mm of rain annually, while the Australian desert typically sees rainfall ranging from 81 to 250 mm per year. Thunderstorms occur frequently in some regions, averaging between 15 to 20 per year. Southern Australia is influenced by westerly winds and rain-bearing cold fronts during winter, while summers are characterized by dry heat with occasional coastal sea breezes. Dry spells in southern and eastern states can lead to bushfires, particularly in Victoria and New South Wales, fueled by hot, dry interior winds. Northern Australia experiences a wet summer due to the monsoon season, typically from October to April, bringing humid north-westerly winds and frequent showers and thunderstorms. Tropical cyclones can also bring heavy rainfall to coastal regions, extending further inland.
